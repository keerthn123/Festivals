Onam Festival

Onam is a major annual festival celebrated in the state of Kerala, India. It is a harvest festival and is celebrated with great enthusiasm, usually between August and September. The festival is marked by various traditional rituals, music, dances, sports, boat races, and a grand feast known as Onasadya.

Onam commemorates the homecoming of the legendary King Mahabali, whose reign was believed to be the golden era of Kerala. According to legend, the devas (gods) were jealous of Mahabali's popularity and approached Lord Vishnu for help. Lord Vishnu, in his Vamana avatar, tricked Mahabali into giving up his kingdom, but granted him the boon of visiting his people once a year. The festival of Onam is a celebration of this annual visit.

Onam lasts for ten days, starting with Atham and ending with Thiruvonam. The highlights of the festival include:

Pookalam: Intricate floral carpets made on the ground in front of homes.

Vallamkali: The famous snake boat race, where teams row traditional longboats in a fierce competition.

Pulikali: A colorful folk art where performers paint their bodies like tigers and dance to traditional music.

Onasadya: A grand vegetarian feast with over 20 dishes, served on banana leaves.

Thumbi Thullal: Traditional dance performances by women in Kerala's rural areas.

Onam is a vibrant and inclusive festival, celebrated by people of all communities in Kerala, and it symbolizes prosperity, unity, and cultural pride.
